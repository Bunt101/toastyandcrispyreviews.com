Review: Sleeping Beauty (1959)

Prince Blue Balls

★★★

Many of the old Disney Princess movies follow a similar pattern, which has hurt them in the long run. However, they're not necessarily bad movies; in fact, they still have a lot of charm and love. Their only problems are predictability, a lack of development of the prince, and a heavy focus on the side characters rather than the main characters. A lot of these old Disney Princess movies follow a similar pattern and that has hurt these films in the long run. They're not necessarily bad movies; in fact, they still hold a lot of charm and love to them. Their only problems are predictability, lack of development of the prince, and heavy focus on the side characters rather than the main characters.

Additionally, the storylines often rely on traditional tropes, such as love at first sight and the idea that a prince can solve all problems. This not only makes the narratives feel outdated but also presents a narrow view of romance and self-worth.

Moreover, while the side characters can be entertaining, they sometimes overshadow the main protagonists' journeys. This imbalance can leave viewers feeling disconnected from the leading characters, as their growth and development may be sidelined in favor of comedic relief or fanciful side stories.

As the animation industry has evolved, many newer films have begun to subvert these patterns by providing stronger character arcs and more complex relationships, allowing audiences to experience deeper connections with the protagonists. This shift highlights the need for stories that embrace individuality and promote a sense of agency, making the characters more relatable and inspiring. Ultimately, while the classic Disney Princess movies hold a special place in many hearts, there's a yearning for narratives that resonate with contemporary values and encourage young viewers to pursue their own dreams and paths.

Rating: 3/4 stars.
